How much did a £250k mortgage cost throughout 2025?

Mortgage rates fluctuated throughout 2025, affecting monthly repayments for homeowners. Here's how a typical £250,000 mortgage on a 30-year term varied month by month.

Using Bank of England data for 2-year fixed rate mortgages at 75% LTV, we've calculated what homeowners with a £250,000 mortgage on a 30-year term would have paid each month throughout 2025.

Key findings

2025 Mortgage Cost Highlights

  • Highest rate: 4.69% in February (£1,291/month)
  • Lowest rate: 4.13% in August (£1,218/month)
  • Average monthly payment: £1,249
  • Total range: £73/month difference between highest and lowest
  • Annual impact: £876 difference between best and worst months

What this means for homeowners

The data shows that 2025 mortgage rates remained relatively stable compared to the volatility seen in 2022-2023, when rates peaked above 6%. However, even the small variations throughout the year demonstrate why timing matters when fixing a mortgage rate.

Those who secured a rate in August 2025 at 4.13% would save £876 annually compared to someone who fixed in February at 4.69% – a meaningful difference over a typical 2 or 5-year fixed term.

Looking ahead

With the Bank of England continuing to adjust policy rates based on inflation data, mortgage rates are expected to remain responsive to economic conditions. Homeowners approaching the end of their fixed-rate term should monitor rates closely and consider securing a new deal 3-6 months in advance.
