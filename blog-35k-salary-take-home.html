Take home pay on a £35k salary UK 2025/26

If you're earning £35,000 per year in the UK, you might be wondering exactly how much money you'll actually take home after tax and National Insurance contributions. Let's break down the numbers for the 2025/26 tax year.

The breakdown

On a £35,000 gross salary, you'll pay:

  • Income Tax: £4,486 per year (£374 per month)
  • National Insurance: £2,564 per year (£214 per month)

This means your annual take-home pay is £27,950, which works out to £2,329 per month or £537 per week.

How is this calculated?

The UK tax system uses a progressive tax structure. With a £35,000 salary, you benefit from the personal allowance of £12,570 (tax-free), then pay 20% basic rate tax on the remaining £22,430.

For National Insurance, you pay 8% on earnings between £12,570 and £50,270, which comes to £2,564 annually on your £35k salary.

What about pension contributions?

If you're enrolled in a workplace pension scheme (which is automatic for most employees), you'll typically contribute 5% of your qualifying earnings. This would reduce your take-home pay by approximately £146 per month, but it's a valuable long-term investment in your retirement.

Student loan deductions

If you have a student loan, you'll also have repayments deducted from your salary. On Plan 2, you'd pay 9% on earnings above £27,295, which would be around £58 per month on a £35k salary.
