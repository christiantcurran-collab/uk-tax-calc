What are Fixed Rate Bonds?

Fixed rate bonds (also known as fixed term deposits) are savings accounts where you lock your money away for a set period in exchange for a guaranteed interest rate. They typically offer higher rates than easy access savings accounts.

Things to Consider

Lock-in Period

Your money is locked away for the full term. Early withdrawal usually means losing interest or paying penalties.

FSCS Protection

Check the account is covered by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) up to £85,000 per person, per institution.

Interest Payment

Interest can be paid annually or at maturity. Annual payments may offer compound growth if reinvested.

Tax on Interest

Interest is taxable but most people have a Personal Savings Allowance (£1,000 for basic rate taxpayers).

Frequently Asked Questions

Are fixed rate bonds safe?

Yes, as long as they're covered by the FSCS. This protects up to £85,000 per person, per financial institution if the provider goes bust.

Can I access my money early?

Most fixed rate bonds don't allow early access. Some providers offer "notice accounts" with fixed rates that allow withdrawals with advance notice, but rates are usually lower.

What happens when my bond matures?

When your bond matures, the provider will usually transfer your money (plus interest) to an easy access account. You'll typically have a short window to reinvest at a new rate or withdraw without penalty.

Should I choose a longer term for a higher rate?

Not necessarily. Longer terms don't always offer better rates, and you lose flexibility. Consider your financial goals and whether you might need access to the money.
