What is an ISA?

An Individual Savings Account (ISA) is a tax-efficient savings or investment account. You don't pay tax on interest or investment gains within an ISA. The annual ISA allowance for 2024/25 is £20,000.

Types of ISAs

Cash ISA

Save cash with guaranteed returns. Interest is tax-free. Best for short-term savings or emergency funds.

Stocks & Shares ISA

Invest in stocks, bonds, and funds. Potential for higher returns but with investment risk. Best for long-term growth.

Lifetime ISA (LISA)

Save for your first home or retirement. Get a 25% government bonus (max £1,000/year). Age 18-39 only.

Innovative Finance ISA

Lend money through peer-to-peer platforms. Potentially higher returns but higher risk. Not covered by FSCS.

ISA Tips & Rules

Annual Allowance

You can save up to £20,000 across all ISAs in the 2024/25 tax year (April to April). Use it or lose it!

Transfers

You can transfer ISAs between providers without losing tax benefits. Always use the official transfer process.

Multiple ISAs

You can open one of each type of ISA per tax year, but your total contributions can't exceed £20,000.

Flexible ISAs

Some ISAs are "flexible" - you can withdraw and replace money in the same tax year without affecting your allowance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I choose a Cash ISA or Stocks & Shares ISA?

Cash ISAs are safer with guaranteed returns, best for short-term savings. Stocks & Shares ISAs offer potentially higher returns but with risk, best for long-term goals (5+ years).

Can I have multiple ISAs?

Yes, you can have multiple ISAs, but you can only open and contribute to one of each type per tax year. Your total contributions across all ISAs can't exceed £20,000.

What happens to my ISA when the tax year ends?

Your ISA continues and remains tax-free. Any unused allowance expires, but you get a fresh £20,000 allowance for the new tax year.

Is a Lifetime ISA worth it?

If you're buying your first home (under £450k) or saving for retirement, the 25% government bonus is excellent. But the 25% withdrawal penalty for other uses makes it inflexible.
